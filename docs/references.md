# References

## Books and Publications

1. **"The Book on Rental Property Investing" by Brandon Turner**
   BiggerPockets Publishing, 2015. Comprehensive guide to building wealth through rental properties, covering acquisition strategies, financing, management, and scaling.

2. **"The Millionaire Real Estate Investor" by Gary Keller**
   McGraw-Hill, 2005. Systematic approach to real estate investing based on interviews with successful millionaire investors.

3. **"What Every Real Estate Investor Needs to Know About Cash Flow... And 36 Other Key Financial Measures" by Frank Gallinelli**
   McGraw-Hill, 2015. Detailed reference for real estate financial metrics and calculations.

4. **BiggerPockets - Real Estate Investing Platform**
   https://www.biggerpockets.com
   Comprehensive resource including podcasts, forums, articles, calculators, and networking for real estate investors at all experience levels.

5. **Investopedia - Real Estate Investing**
   https://www.investopedia.com/real-estate-investing-4689764

## Government Resources

7. **IRS Publication 527 - Residential Rental Property**
   https://www.irs.gov/publications/p527
   Official IRS guidance on tax treatment of rental income, depreciation, expenses, and disposition of rental properties.

8. **IRS Publication 544 - Sales and Other Dispositions of Assets**
   https://www.irs.gov/publications/p544
   Guidance on capital gains, depreciation recapture, and 1031 exchanges for investment property sales.

9. **U.S. Census Bureau - Housing Data**
   https://www.census.gov/topics/housing.html
   Demographic and housing data for market analysis including population trends, household composition, and housing characteristics.

10. **Bureau of Labor Statistics - Local Area Unemployment Statistics**
    https://www.bls.gov/lau/
    Employment and unemployment data by metropolitan area for market economic analysis.

11. **Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED)**
    https://fred.stlouisfed.org
    Economic data including interest rates, housing price indices, construction spending, and mortgage rates.
